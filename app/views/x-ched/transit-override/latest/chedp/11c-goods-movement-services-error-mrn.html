What is the CTC?

The Common Transit Convention (CTC) is a customs procedure that allows you to quickly move goods between countries in:

  • the UK
  • the EU
  • other CTC countries

It can help reduce the number of customs declarations you'll need to complete and you won't have to pay charges until you end the movement.

To move your goods using the CTC, you need to:

  1. check if you can use it
  2. find out how to use it to move goods
  3. plan your route before you use it

Will the transport use the Goods Vehicle Movement Service (GVMS)?

What is GVMS?

GVMS helps to speed up customs clearance at some UK ports.

To move your goods, you need to:

  1. Find out which ports use GVMS (opens in new tab)
  2. Register for GVMS (opens in new tab)
  3. Get a goods movement reference (GMR) (opens in new tab)

The GMR is a single reference that brings together multiple declarations. Without it, you cannot move your goods through UK ports which use GVMS and you may face delays.

The driver must check GVMS to find out if an inspection is required before they disembark.
